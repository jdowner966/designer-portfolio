Flag-A-Mania

Flag-A-Mania is a group capstone project designed in my final year at the University of Central Florida.

Starting out as a prototype for a country flag-guessing game, it slowly evolved into a more ambitious geographical website.

The primary Flag Trivia mode is where players are meant to guess the correct flags within a time limit.

Based on PHP, the page pulls country names and flag images from a MySQL database and uses JavaScript to check for correct answers (whether the country flag matches the name in the database).

The Country Facts mode is where players answer trivia questions about certain countries. Also seen here: you can adjust the settings for both modes, like the number of rounds before playing.

This mode utilizes the same technologies as the Flag Trivia mode, pulling questions from another MySQL table.

But Flag-A-Mania doesn't simply stop at being a game. It even has a Simple Phrases page where you can check a variety of phrases in a country's native language. Partially inspired by Duolingo, as you can probably tell.

The displaying of different phrases based on what tab you click on, as well as what page based on what country you select, is all the work of JavaScript Objects. None of these countries have their own separate page.

Color scheme too bright? Not to worry. Flag-A-Mania has you covered with a dark mode. Just click the moon icon in the top right-hand corner, and click the sun if you ever desire to change it back!
